# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A PIP is a structured, time-boxed intervention for when an employee is not meeting expectations and informal coaching has not moved the needle. It is a last resort, not a first response. Done well, it creates clarity and a fair chance to succeed. Done poorly, it is a paper trail to an exit. Know which one you are doing.

---

## Before You Start

A PIP should not come as a surprise. If it does, something went wrong earlier. Before initiating one, check:

- Have you given specific, written feedback about the performance gap?
- Has the person had a reasonable opportunity to address it?
- Are expectations clearly documented somewhere they can reference?
- Have you ruled out systemic causes — unclear requirements, team friction, tooling gaps, personal circumstances?

If the answers are no, start there. A PIP without this groundwork will fail and likely create legal and morale risk.

**Loop in HR and/or your manager before initiating.** This is not optional.

---

## When to Use a PIP

A PIP is appropriate when:

- Performance gaps are persistent and documented over weeks or months, not days
- You have given direct feedback and coaching with no meaningful change
- The gap is in execution or behavior, not a mismatch of role fit (which is a different conversation)
- You genuinely believe improvement is possible and you are willing to invest in it

A PIP is not appropriate as:

- A first response to a performance issue
- A way to force an outcome you have already decided on
- A substitute for a role mismatch or layoff conversation

---

## Structure of a PIP

### 1. Clear Statement of the Gap
Be specific about what is not meeting expectations. Anchor it to observable behavior and outcomes, not traits.

- **Weak:** "You need to communicate better."
- **Strong:** "In the last three sprints, your standup updates have regularly omitted blockers until they escalated. In the last retro, two teammates noted they were surprised by delays that could have been raised earlier."

### 2. Expectations Going Forward
What does success look like? Be concrete and measurable.

- "Blockers are surfaced within 24 hours of identification, in Slack or standup."
- "Standup updates include what you did, what you are doing, and any blockers."

### 3. Support You Will Provide
What are you doing to help? This is a commitment from you, not just demands on them.

- Weekly 1:1 check-ins with explicit agenda focused on the PIP areas
- Pairing time or access to specific resources
- Clear escalation path if they feel blocked

### 4. Timeline
- Typical duration: 30–90 days
- Milestone check-in at the midpoint
- Clear end date with defined outcomes

### 5. Outcomes
State explicitly what happens at the end:
- Expectations met → PIP closes, you continue working together
- Expectations not met → employment ends or role changes

Do not be vague here. The person deserves to know the stakes.

---

## The Conversation

This conversation is hard. Have it in person (or video, if remote). Do not send a PIP document as a first notification.

- Be direct about the situation and the stakes
- Avoid softening language that obscures the seriousness ("I just wanted to share some thoughts…")
- Give them space to respond — this is a dialogue, not a monologue
- Do not try to make them feel better at the expense of clarity

After the conversation, follow up in writing the same day.

---

## During the PIP

- Check in weekly. Do not disappear and reappear at the end date.
- Document your observations — what you saw, when, and what you discussed.
- Acknowledge progress explicitly when you see it. This is not just about catching failure.
- If something changes materially (positive or negative), address it directly and update the written record.

---

## Closing a PIP

**If expectations are met:** Close it explicitly. Say it out loud and in writing. Do not let success go unacknowledged.

**If expectations are not met:** Work with HR on the offboarding process. The documentation you have built during the PIP matters here — do not improvise.

---

## Notes

- Most PIPs do not result in the person turning around. If you are running one, be honest with yourself about what you are expecting.
- The people around the individual on the PIP will notice something is happening. Keep things confidential but do not pretend nothing is going on.
- If you are running multiple PIPs at once, that is a signal about something larger — hiring, onboarding, expectations-setting, or team health.
